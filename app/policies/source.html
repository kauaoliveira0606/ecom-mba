Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 20, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Ecom MBA collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when visiting the website, landing pages, and related digital properties, and when interacting with information products, webinars, workshops, and services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

Registration & Lead Capture includes name, email address, phone number, company name and industry, job title and experience level, and information submitted through forms, surveys, or questionnaires.

Webinar & Workshop Registration collects registration details, attendance and engagement data, questions submitted during live events, and feedback responses.

Purchase & Payment Information includes billing name and address, payment method details processed through third-party processors, purchase history, and refund communications.

Communications encompasses email inquiries, chat messages, customer service interactions, feedback, testimonials, reviews, and survey responses.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

Website Usage Data tracks IP address and device identifiers, browser type and operating system, pages visited and time spent, referral source and exit pages, and search queries.

Cookies & Tracking Technologies include session cookies, persistent cookies, pixel tags, web beacons, and local storage technologies.

Analytics & Performance captures heatmaps, video engagement metrics, form completion rates, and device performance data.

Email Engagement monitors open rates, click-through rates, delivery status, and preference data.

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Primary Uses

Information supports service delivery, transactional and marketing communications, experience personalization, analytics and improvement, lead nurturing, customer support, and legal compliance.

3.2 Marketing & Promotional Uses

The company sends promotional emails, creates audience segments for targeted advertising, conducts A/B testing, builds lookalike audiences, and tracks conversion attribution.

4. Information Sharing & Disclosure

4.0 No Sale or Transfer of Personal Data

We do not sell, share, rent, or transfer your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. Data is shared only for service fulfillment, business transfers, legal requirements, or with explicit consent.

4.1 Third-Party Service Providers

Information is shared with vendors assisting in operations including email platforms, payment processors, analytics tools, webinar hosts, support systems, and cloud providers.

4.2 Legal Requirements & Protection

Disclosure occurs when legally required, to enforce terms, protect against fraud, or safeguard rights and safety.

4.3 Business Transfers

Information may transfer during mergers, acquisitions, or asset sales with prior notice.

4.4 Aggregated & De-Identified Data

Anonymized data may be shared with partners for research and analytics purposes.

5. Data Retention

Lead data is retained for three years from last engagement or until unsubscription. Customer data persists for relationship duration plus seven years for compliance. Email engagement data is kept for two years. Website analytics are retained for 26 months. Support communications are kept for two years after resolution. Deletion requests are processed within 30 days unless retention is legally required.

6. Your Privacy Rights & Choices

6.1 Access & Portability

Users may request personal information copies, portable data formats, and corrections to inaccurate information.

6.2 Opt-Out & Unsubscribe

Email marketing can be opted out via unsubscribe links. Cookies are manageable through browser settings. Targeted advertising opt-outs are available through industry tools.

6.3 California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have rights to know collected information, delete data, opt-out of sales, and receive non-discrimination for exercising rights.

6.4 European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

EU/EEA individuals have rights to access, correct, and delete information, restrict processing, request portability, withdraw consent, and lodge complaints.

7. Data Security

Security measures include SSL/TLS encryption, secure password hashing, regular audits, access controls, firewalls, and employee training. Breaches trigger notification within 30 days.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Essential cookies manage sessions and functionality. Performance cookies support analytics and optimization. Marketing cookies enable retargeting and conversion tracking. Third-party cookies from Google Analytics, Facebook, and LinkedIn are utilized.

9. Third-Party Links & Services

The policy does not cover third-party websites and services users should review their own privacy policies.

10. Policy Updates

Policy updates are communicated via website posting, date changes, and email notifications for material changes. Continued use indicates acceptance.

11. Jurisdiction-Specific Provisions

The policy complies with U.S. laws including CCPA, COPPA, and CAN-SPAM; EU GDPR; and Canadian PIPEDA regulations.
